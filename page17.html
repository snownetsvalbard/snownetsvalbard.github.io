What is snow?

How does snow form those incredible snow crystals?

What is snow made of?

Snow is made of air and ice, yes although only when it is pure! Fresh snow can be made of fractal particles (dendritic crystals, the one you love skiing on, see pictures), but also of very different shapes such as rounded, needles, columns, gresil (the one destroying wine grapes for examples). Snow is a very good media to monitor the changes of climate, simply because snow is very sensitive to atmospheric parameters like wind and temperature. Did you ever slide on melting snow on ground, we too!

Snow metamorphoses over time

Snow is an unstable media, in constant evolution once on ground, as a function of the so-called snow metamorphism. The latter is stronger when the temperature gradient is stronger (difference between the upper and bottom snow layer temperature) and can change a rounded particle into a fully faceted shape (like a gobelet, scientifically called depth hoar, see pictures), and in just two weeks! This is impressive, and so beautiful when observing such shapes.

Snow is an unstable media, in constant evolution

The beautiful properties of snow

The shape and size of the snow crystals are the main physical parameters driving the physical properties of snow, and these are: density, albedo (or ability to reflect incoming sunlight), thermal conductivity (or the way snow conducts energy, like a fripan on your electric plate), and of course the way snow evolves over time will drive the way it melts, and disappears.

Snow responds fast but never the same way to climate change

Snow is a fantastic media to study climate change, not only because it evolves and responds rapidly to it, but also because snow is at the interface of most of the geo-spheres in the Polar Regions. Geo-sphere can be for example the ocean, the permafrost, a glacier, or simply the atmosphere. If you look around in the Polar Regions, snow is always sitting in between one of two connected spheres (ground/glacier and atmosphere for example). That simply means any transfer of energy, weather radiative (sunlight) or conductive (heat) as to go through snow. It makes snow a critical media to study, understand, and follow up in order to get more knowledge in the processes involved regarding climate warming and energy exchange in the Polar areas, in other words, climate and climate change.
