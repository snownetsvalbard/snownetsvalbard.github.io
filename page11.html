Istituto di Scienze Polari (ISP- CNR), Venice 

The Institute of Polar Science in Venice, in collaboration with the Ca Foscari University of Venice are specialised in the analysis of trace impurities from different environments such as snow, ice, sea water, ground and aerosol (atmosphere) in the Arctic, Antarctic and high alpine regions. The main research focus of the laboratory are chemistry, geochemistry (elemental and isotopic analysis) and the physics of polar environments with the aim to reconstruct and interpret paleoclimatic events and understand transport/accumulation of pollutants.

Tracing chemical elements and pollution
Part of the activity is aimed at the development of high sensitivity analytical methods for the determination of inorganic and organic chemical tracers using mass spectrometric techniques, and for the quantification and identification of the polymers in microplastics. The current research themes have a multidisciplinary approach covering many chemical/geochemical as well as physical aspects. 

Environmental research
The scientific activities take a holistic approach to polar environments by relating them to non-polar areas that may interact with them such as mountains and other remote areas. In this context, attention is paid to the study of different environments: air, water (marine, lake, melt waters), snow, ice, sediments, soils, and permafrost, also through the combined use of data and models. In addition to field-based studies, the main atmospheric physical parameters and processes are studied to understand the process occuring and the role of the atmosphere controlling the impurities present within the ground (snow, ice, permafrost and in last case biota)
